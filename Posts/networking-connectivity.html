Quiz

1. The main Google server is in the United States. When you use Google to find the answer to a question, what transmission medium is used? (Select from satellite transmission, undersea fibre optic cable, copper telephone cables.) [1]

Undersea fibre optic cable

2. (a) Define the term bandwidth. [1]

The amount of data that can be sent/received at one time

(b) How long would it take to download a 700 Megabyte file on a 40Mb connection? [1]

140 seconds ( (700 x 8)/40 )

3. (a) Explain the difference between uploading and downloading data from the Internet. [2]

Uploading is generally slower and is where information is sent to the internet. Whereas downloading is usually faster and means that things from the internet are put on your computer.

(b) Why is uploading generally much slower than downloading? [2]

Uploading is generally slower than downloading because this is what the service provider chooses. They choose for download speed to be greater than the upload speed because more people download items than to upload.

4. (a) Explain what is meant by buffering and why it is used when you download a film from the Internet. [2]

Buffering occurs when the download speed of the file is slower than the play speed, this results in the computer trying to play something that hasn't downloaded, meaning that the user will have to wait for it to download.

(b) Explain why you may sometimes get a message “Loading…” while you are watching a movie online. [1]

This means that the video is buffering and requires the user to wait for the video to download.
